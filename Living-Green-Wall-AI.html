Living Green Walls and AI: Designing Human Centered Smart Bio Interface for Urban Environment

This project was developed as part of a university research.

The rapid urbanization of modern cities has intensified environmental and social challenges, driving the need for innovative solutions like living green walls. While AI-enhanced vertical gardens demonstrate technical proficiency in plant maintenance and environmental monitoring, their potential as platforms for meaningful human-nature interaction remains underexplored. This study investigates how human engagement with AI-enhanced living green walls can be improved through human-computer interaction (HCI) design. Using a qualitative approach, we developed a 1mÃ—1m prototype integrating IoT sensors, voice interaction, and real-time feedback. Data from 13 participants were analyzed thematically, revealing six key themes: seamless nature-tech integration, emotional impact (surprise-to-connection journey), anthropomorphic perception (e.g., attributing personality to the system),usability challenges, urban implementation potential, and ethical concerns (privacy, authenticity). Findings suggest that AI green walls transcend functional roles, acting as relational technologies that foster emotional bonds and psychological restoration. Participants valued intuitive interfaces, responsive feedback, and biophilic aesthetics but emphasized the need for transparency in data usage. The study proposes design principles such as dynamic personalization, emotive responsiveness, and inclusive accessibility to bridge the gap between technological efficiency and human-centred experience. This research contributes to sustainable HCI by reframing green walls as interactive social interfaces, offering actionable insights for urban planners, designers, and policymakers seeking to enhance ecological engagement in smart cities.
